Qonaqasy: The Art of Hospitality

Traditional Kazakh hospitality is memorable to anyone who has visited Kazakhstan. Rooted deeply in their nomadic past, welcoming guests with open arms and the finest delicacies was a sacred duty.

  • Konaqykade: At the end of a feast, the host asks the guest to perform a song or play an instrument, bridging connections through music.
  • Erulik: A beautiful tradition of welcoming new neighbors to the village (aul) with a hearty meal to help them integrate into the society.
  • Shashu: A joyful custom of showering guests with sweets and coins during momentous occasions, symbolizing abundance and good luck.

Important Life Milestones

Children are considered the greatest treasure in a Kazakh family. Numerous customs mark their growth and development, preparing them for the challenges of life on the steppe.

  • Tusau Kesu: The "cutting of the ties" ceremony takes place when a child takes its first steps. Their legs are loosely tied with a black and white string, which is then cut by a respected elder to ensure a successful path forward.
  • Besikke Salu: The ceremonial placing of the newborn into the traditional cradle (besik), accompanied by blessings and celebrations.
  • Kyz Uzatu: A heartfelt and emotional tradition where the bride's parents host a farewell feast before she departs to join her husband's family.

Nauryz & Festivals

A tradition dating back to ancient pre-Islamic times, Nauryz is the festival of spring, renewal of nature, and the beginning of a new year, celebrated on the vernal equinox.

  • Nauryz Kozhe: A special festive drink made of seven ingredients, representing the seven days of the week and universal eternity.
  • Aitys: A mandatory part of celebrations, Aitys is an improvisational musical and poetic competition between two akyns (bards) playing the dombra.
  • Equestrian Games: Festivities are often accompanied by traditional games like Baiga (horse racing), demonstrating the deep bond between nomads and horses.
